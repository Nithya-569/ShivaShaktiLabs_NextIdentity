🚨 Problem Statement

Many individuals from underserved communities face significant challenges in accessing stable opportunities, guidance, and support systems.

Despite the presence of:

Government welfare schemes
Skill development and training programs
Community-led support initiatives

there remains a disconnect between available resources and people who need them.

For example:

In Karnataka, transgender individuals receive approximately ₹800–₹1,200 per month under the Mythri Pension Scheme
The 2025–26 budget allocates ₹2.54 crore for pensions and ₹3 crore for rehabilitation

However, financial support alone is not sufficient for sustainable livelihoods.

⚠️ Core Gap

The primary issue is not the absence of support systems, but the lack of:

Centralized access to opportunities and services
Awareness about existing programs
Proper guidance and mentorship
Connectivity between individuals and support networks

As a result:

Many individuals are unaware of available opportunities
Struggle to find direction or guidance
Cannot connect with relevant communities or mentors
Face difficulty in becoming financially independent

👉 Key Insight:
“The problem is not lack of resources — it is lack of access and connection.”

💡 Proposed Solution — NextIdentity

NextIdentity is a web-based social support platform designed to bridge this gap by creating a unified and accessible ecosystem.

The platform connects:

Community → Safe space for interaction and support
Opportunities → Jobs, skill development, and growth pathways
Support Systems → Guidance, mentorship, and resources
